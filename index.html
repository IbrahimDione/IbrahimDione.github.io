Area of research

My research focuses on numerical analysis and scientific computing, particularly boundary/interface approximation methods related to fluid-structure interaction and solid contact/friction. My work encompasses theoretical aspects, such as stability and accuracy proofs, as well as applied aspects involving interdisciplinary and industrial applications. My research primarily applies to solid and fluid mechanics, as well as biomechanics.
I am also interested in the mathematics of machine and deep learning, where interesting topics exist in collaboration with industry. For example, from July 2023 to June 2025, I collaborated with Black Arcs, an IT infrastructure, data processing, and hosting technology company. There, I used agent-based mathematical predictive models to create interactive analytics software and foster a deep understanding of the impact of our decisions.
